Case Study

Project Summary

CommBank is required to regularly review and update customer identity information to meet KYC regulatory obligations. Historically, this process often required branch visits, creating friction for customers and significant operational overhead for staff. KYC compliance is critical: failure to complete checks can result in account restrictions and erode trust.

Strategic Foundation

Experience Strategy

We knew our customers: business owners and operators who needed to complete KYC without disrupting their day. That clarity shaped my approach. Before designing, I established decision lenses to evaluate every choice:

⏸️

Design for interruption

Assume people will pause and resume. The flow had to support multi-session from the start.

✅

Completion over compliance theatre

We optimised for completion, not just the audit. Multi-session support was core.

🧭

Complexity as structure, not content

Content is fixed by regulation. The problem is how the journey feels. We tackled perceived effort through structure: chunking, progress, control.

🔁

Reuse as a design criterion

Design patterns that scale to other flows. Every pattern was evaluated for use beyond KYC.

Evidence-based Design

Validation & Insights

I led validation with existing customers completing realistic verification scenarios. Three themes emerged that shaped the solution: trust, flow structure, and language.

💡 Key Learnings

  • Trust: Customers needed reassurance that the request was legitimate. Clear branding and calm language mattered.
  • Structure: Progress visibility reduced frustration in longer flows. I identified Path as the right structural solution and drove its adoption for KYC.
  • Language: Softer copy improved willingness to engage and informed changes across the flow.

A product decision

Introducing:
Path

Path is CommBank's design-system pattern for long, compliance-driven flows. It breaks journeys into clear steps, shows progress, and supports pause-and-resume, exactly what KYC needed. Unlike one-question-at-a-time approaches, Path gives customers a map of the whole journey while keeping each step focused.

It was built for another business banking product and already supported both individual and business verification. I proposed we adopt it for KYC, aligned stakeholders, and we shipped on Path. The animations below show how it works.

Design Craft

Key Product Design Decisions

Four decisions where I influenced direction:

⚖️

Balanced compliance with clarity

Regulatory language caused confusion in validation. I pushed back with evidence, influenced Content and stakeholders to simplify terminology, and maintained full compliance.

🌏

Designed for real-world completion

Validation showed customers couldn't always complete KYC in one sitting. I championed multi-session completion, negotiated the technical trade-offs with Engineering, and secured buy-in.

🔔

Framed KYC as a reminder, not a warning

Alarmist language increased anxiety in validation. I drove changes to copy and visual treatment, softening tone to encourage completion without reducing regulatory urgency.

👀

Reduced perceived effort in a long journey

KYC required many steps. I advocated for a structural approach to reduce perceived effort, turning it into a series of clear steps. This led to the adoption of Path when validation surfaced the need.

Looking back

Reflections & What's Next

What I Learned

  • Validation evidence is the best way to influence stakeholders on compliance flows
  • Language and tone matter as much as structure when customers are anxious
  • Reusing patterns like Path speeds delivery without sacrificing quality

What I'd Do Next

  • Clarify why KYC is needed and what happens if it's not completed
  • Run accessibility audits for screen reader and keyboard navigation
  • Extend Path to other compliance flows and document reuse patterns
